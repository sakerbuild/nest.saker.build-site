Privacy policy

This document describes how saker.nest handles information that is related to you.

Personal information

We store the e-mail address that you've provided to us when your account was created or provided later as a modification. We don't share this with anyone.

Collected data

We don't collect usage related data. We may collect technical information about your interaction with the saker.nest repository. These may include: IP address of your login, web browser type, operating system type.

We don't track users, don't show ads and don't personalize the site in any way.

Cookies

We store the following essential cookies in the localStorage:

  • <api-domain-name>nest_session_key: The API key that is used with the backend server after you've logged in to the site.
  • <api-domain-name>nest_session_secret: The API secret that is used with the backend server after you've logegd in to the site.
  • dark_theme: A boolean that stores the choosen theme for the site. If you never use the 'Switch to theme' feature of the site, this item is never set.

Your content

The packages, bundles, and other resources that you upload to the site is your own. You provide them to us to freely distribute them without limitation.

After you upload and publish a package, anyone will be able to download it. The uploaded bundles shouldn't contain any personal data or other confidental information. After publishing a bundle you should consider it as if the entire internet knew about it as other agents may mirror the contents of the saker.nest repository.

You cannot unpublish an accidentally published bundle.

Copyright violations

If somebody uploaded your content without your permission, write an e-mail to support@saker.build.

Third party services used by saker.nest

We use the following services to run the saker.nest repository:

  • Google App Engine: Hosts the repository backend. We store all of your uploaded contents via Google App Engine.
  • Mailgun: Used for sending and receiving e-mails. Your e-mail address is shared with Mailgun.
  • GitHub pages: Hosts the static webpage for the repository.
